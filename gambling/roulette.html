Modelling European Roulette¶

Video lecture: https://www.youtube.com/w2-InizN_ZQ

Rules: https://www.casino.org/roulette/odds/

Assuming that the roulette is fair and each bet is iid. Let \(p\) be the prability of winning, \(q\) be the opposite. Let \(X\) be the rewards of winning for betting one dollar, or \(-1\) if loses.

Simple Strategies¶

Single number betting (Straight-up)¶

\(X=35\) when \(p=\frac{1}{37}\), or \(X=-1\) when loses.

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} E(X) & = 35\times p - q = \frac{35}{37} - \frac{36}{37} = - \frac{1}{37} = -0.02702702702 \\ E(X^2) & = 35^2 \times p + q = \frac{35^2}{37} + \frac{36}{37} = 34.0810810811 \\ Var(X) & = E(X^2) - E(X)^2 = 34.0810810811 - (-0.02702702702)^2 = 34.0803506209 \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Two numbers betting (Split)¶

\(X=17\) when \(p=\frac{2}{37}\), or \(X=-1\) when loses.

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} E(X) & = 17 \times p - q = 17 \times \frac{2}{37} - \frac{35}{37} = -0.02702702702 \\ E(X^2) & = 17^2 \times p + q = \frac{17^2 \times 2}{37} + \frac{35}{37} = 16.5675675676 \\ Var(X) & = E(X^2) - E(X)^2 = 16.5675675676 - (-0.02702702702)^2 = 16.5668371074 \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Three numbers betting (Street)¶

\(X=11\) when \(p=\frac{3}{37}\), or \(X=-1\) when loses.

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} E(X) & = 11 \times p - q = 11 \times \frac{3}{37} - \frac{34}{37} = -0.02702702702 \\ E(X^2) & = 17^2 \times p + q = \frac{11^2 \times 3}{37} + \frac{34}{37} = 10.7297297297 \\ Var(X) & = E(X^2) - E(X)^2 = 10.7297297297 - (-0.02702702702)^2 = 10.7289992695 \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Four numbers betting (Corner)¶

\(X=8\) when \(p=\frac{4}{37}\), or \(X=-1\) when loses.

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} E(X) & = 8 \times p - q = 8 \times \frac{4}{37} - \frac{33}{37} = -0.02702702702 \\ E(X^2) & = 8^2 \times p + q = \frac{8^2 \times 4}{37} + \frac{33}{37} = 7.81081081081 \\ Var(X) & = E(X^2) - E(X)^2 = 7.81081081081 - (-0.02702702702)^2 = 7.81008035062 \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Five numbers betting (Basket)¶

Not available for European roulette. But if you see this in a European roulette online, remember, never bet it on. because the expected outcomes are worser.

Six numbers betting (Line)¶

\(X=5\) when \(p=\frac{6}{37}\), or \(X=-1\) when loses.

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} E(X) & = 5 \times p - q = 5 \times \frac{6}{37} - \frac{31}{37} = -0.02702702702 \\ E(X^2) & = 5^2 \times p + q = \frac{5^2 \times 6}{37} + \frac{31}{37} = 4.89189189189 \\ Var(X) & = E(X^2) - E(X)^2 = 4.89189189189 - (-0.02702702702)^2 = 4.8911614317 \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Twelve numbers betting (Column, Dozens)¶

\(X=2\) when \(p=\frac{12}{37}\), or \(X=-1\) when loses.

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} E(X) & = 2 \times p - q = 2 \times \frac{12}{37} - \frac{25}{37} = -0.02702702702 \\ E(X^2) & = 2^2 \times p + q = \frac{2^2 \times 12}{37} + \frac{25}{37} = 1.97297297297 \\ Var(X) & = E(X^2) - E(X)^2 = 1.97297297297 - (-0.02702702702)^2 = 1.97224251278 \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Eighteen numbers betting (Red/Black, Even/Odd, Low/High)¶

\(X=1\) when \(p=\frac{18}{37}\), or \(X=-1\) when loses.

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} E(X) & = 1 \times p - q = 1 \times \frac{18}{37} - \frac{19}{37} = -0.02702702702 \\ E(X^2) & = 1^2 \times p + q = \frac{1^2 \times 18}{37} + \frac{19}{37} = 1 \\ Var(X) & = E(X^2) - E(X)^2 = 1 - (-0.02702702702)^2 = 0.99926953981 \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Complex Strategies¶

Black-Col-3 or Red-Col-2 Strategy¶

Betting on All blacks + betting on Column 3 (No.36)

  • If \(X\) lands on Column 3 and Red color: \(X=1\) and \(p_1=\frac{8}{37}\).

  • If \(X\) lands on Column 3 and Black color: \(X=3\) and \(p_2=\frac{4}{37}\)

  • If \(X\) lands on Non-column 3 but Black: \(X=0\) and \(p_3=\frac{14}{37}\)

  • Otherwise you lose, with \(X=-2\) and \(q=1-p_1-p_2-p_3=\frac{11}{37}\)

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} E(X) & = 1 \times p_1 + 3 \times p_2 + 1 * p_3 - 2 \times q = \frac{8}{37} + \frac{12}{37} - \frac{22}{37} = -\frac{2}{37} = -0.05405405405 \\ E(X^2) & = 1 \times p_1 + 9 \times p_2 + 4 \times q = \frac{8}{37} + \frac{36}{37} + \frac{44}{37} = \frac{88}{37} = 2.37837837838 \\ Var(X) & = E(X^2) - E(X)^2 = 2.37837837838 - 0.05405405405^2 = 2.37545653762 \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Same strategy works when you bet on all Reds + betting on Column 2(No.35)

There’s no need to calculate any other complex strategies. As any complex strategies can be regarded as an additive effects of simple strategies, therefore \(E(X=\sum_{k=1}^nS_k)\).
