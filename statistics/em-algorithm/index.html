Problem Desription

Let X′=[X1,X2,...,Xn]X' = [X_1, X_2,..., X_{n}]X′=[X1​,X2​,...,Xn​] be observed sample, iid with pdf f(x∣θ)f(x|\theta)f(x∣θ). Let Z′=[Z1,Z2,...,Zn]Z'=[Z_1, Z_2,..., Z_{n}]Z′=[Z1​,Z2​,...,Zn​] be hidden/unobserved. We further assume that {Xi}\{ X_i \}{Xi​} and {Zj}\{ Z_j \}{Zj​} are mutually independent.

Let g(x∣θ)g(\bold{x}|\theta)g(x∣θ) be the joint pdf of X\bold{X}X.

Let h(x,z∣θ)h(\bold{x},\bold{z}|\theta)h(x,z∣θ) be the complete pdf of X,Z\bold{X}, \bold{Z}X,Z.

Let k(z∣θ,x)k(\bold{z}|\theta,\bold{x})k(z∣θ,x) be the conditional pdf of the hidden data given the observed data.

k(z∣θ,x)=h(x,z∣θ)g(x∣θ)k(\bold{z}|\theta,\bold{x}) = \frac{h(\bold{x},\bold{z}|\theta)}{g(\bold{x}|\theta)} k(z∣θ,x)=g(x∣θ)h(x,z∣θ)​

We want to find an estimate of the unknown parameter θ\thetaθ.

We'll conduct MLE to maximize the probability of observing XXX by maximizing g(x∣θ)g(\bold{x}|\theta)g(x∣θ). However, the missing data {Zi}\{ Z_i \}{Zi​} yields g(x∣θ)g(\bold{x}|\theta)g(x∣θ) with more unknown parameters.

McLachlan and Krishman (1997) gives a in-depth knowledge of an algorithm call EM Algo. Let's see the inspiration of EM algo. We want to maximize incomplete likelyhood function L(θ;x)=g(x∣θ)L(\theta; \bold{x})=g(\bold{x}|\theta)L(θ;x)=g(x∣θ) wrt θ\thetaθ:

log⁡L(θ;x)=log⁡g(x∣θ)=∫log⁡g(x∣θ)k(z∣θ0,x)dz=∫[log⁡h(x,z∣θ)−log⁡k(z∣θ,x)]k(z∣θ0,x)dz=∫log⁡h(x,z∣θ)k(z∣θ0,x)dz−∫[log⁡k(z∣θ,x)]k(z∣θ0,x)dz=EZ[log⁡h(x,Z∣θ)∣θ0,x]−EZ[log⁡k(Z∣θ,x)∣θ0,x]\begin{aligned} \log{L(\theta; \bold{x})} = \log{g(\bold{x}|\theta)} &= \int{\log{g(\bold{x}|\theta)} k(\bold{z}|\theta_0,\bold{x}) d\bold{z} } \\ &= \int{[\log{h(\bold{x}, \bold{z}|\theta) - \log{k(\bold{z}|\theta, \bold{x})}}] k(\bold{z}|\theta_0,\bold{x}) } d\bold{z} \\ &= \int{\log{h(\bold{x}, \bold{z}|\theta) k(\bold{z}|\theta_0,\bold{x}) } d\bold{z} - \int{[\log{k(\bold{z}|\theta, \bold{x})}}] k(\bold{z}|\theta_0,\bold{x}) } d\bold{z} \\ &= E_{\bold{Z}}[\log{h(\bold{x},\bold{Z}|\theta)|\theta_0,\bold{x}}] - E_{\bold{Z}}[\log{k(\bold{Z}|\theta, \bold{x})}|\theta_0,\bold{x}] \end{aligned} logL(θ;x)=logg(x∣θ)​=∫logg(x∣θ)k(z∣θ0​,x)dz=∫[logh(x,z∣θ)−logk(z∣θ,x)]k(z∣θ0​,x)dz=∫logh(x,z∣θ)k(z∣θ0​,x)dz−∫[logk(z∣θ,x)]k(z∣θ0​,x)dz=EZ​[logh(x,Z∣θ)∣θ0​,x]−EZ​[logk(Z∣θ,x)∣θ0​,x]​

Here we are assuming θ0\theta_0θ0​ is known. The L(θ;x,z)=h(x,Z∣θ)∣θ0,xL(\theta; \bold{x}, \bold{z}) = h(\bold{x},\bold{Z}|\theta)|\theta_0,\bold{x}L(θ;x,z)=h(x,Z∣θ)∣θ0​,x is the complete likelyhood function.

Let

Q(θ∣θ0,x)=EZ[log⁡h(x,Z∣θ)∣θ0,x]Q(\theta|\theta_0,\bold{x})=E_{\bold{Z}}[\log{h(\bold{x},\bold{Z}|\theta)|\theta_0,\bold{x}}] Q(θ∣θ0​,x)=EZ​[logh(x,Z∣θ)∣θ0​,x]
