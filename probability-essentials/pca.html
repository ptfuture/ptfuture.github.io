Principal Components Analysis¶

Let \(\mathbf{X} \sim N(\mathbf{\mu, \Sigma})\). Then $\( \Lambda = \Gamma \Sigma \Gamma' \)$

Assume \(\lambda_1 \ge \lambda_2 \ge ... \ge \lambda_1 \gt 0\)

Then

\[ \mathbf{Y}=\Gamma(X-\mu) \sim N(\mathbf{0, \Lambda}) \]

\(Y\) is call the vector of principal components. And: $\( \Gamma = \begin{bmatrix} \vec{v_1}' \\ \vec{v_2}' \\ ... \\ \vec{v_n}' \end{bmatrix} \)$

where \(\vec{v_i}\) are the orthonormal eigenvector basis.

Total variance¶

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} \tau(X) & = \sum_{i=1}^n\sigma^2 = trace(\Sigma) \\ & = trace(\Gamma'\Lambda\Gamma) = trace(\Lambda\Gamma\Gamma') \\ & = \sum_{i=1}^n\lambda_i = trace(\Lambda) \\ & = \tau(Y) \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Principal Component¶

For all any \(\mathbf{a} = \sum_1^na_i\mathbf{v_i}\), such that \(\lVert\mathbf{a}\rVert^2 = 1\)

\[ Var(\mathbf{a'X}) \le Var(Y_1) \]

Proof:

Since \(v_i\) are orthonormal basis, for

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} Var(\mathbf{\mathbf{a'}X}) & = \mathbf{a'} \Sigma a \\ & = [\mathbf{a'v_1}, \mathbf{a'v_2},\dots,\mathbf{a'v_n}] \Lambda \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{v_1'a} \\ \mathbf{v_2'a} \\ \dots \\ \mathbf{v_n'a} \end{bmatrix} \\ & = [\lambda_1 \mathbf{a'v_1}, \lambda_2 \mathbf{a'v_2},\dots, \lambda_n \mathbf{a'v_n}]\begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{v_1'a} \\ \mathbf{v_2'a} \\ \dots \\ \mathbf{v_n'a} \end{bmatrix} \\ & = \sum_1^n \lambda_i (\mathbf{a'v_i})^2 \\ & = \sum_1^n \lambda_i a_i^2 \le \lambda_1 = Var(Y_1) \end{aligned} \end{split}\]
