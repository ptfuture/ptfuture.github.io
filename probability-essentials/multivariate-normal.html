Multivariate Normal¶

Video lecture: https://youtu.be/7fWUvqgUt2Y

Covariance¶

Theorom¶

\(Cov(AX) = A Cov(X) A'\)

Proof:

\[\begin{split}\begin{aligned} Cov(AX) & = E[(AX-A\mu)(AX-A\mu)'] \\ & = E[ AXX'A' - AX\mu'A' - A\mu X'A' + A\mu \mu' A'] \\ & = AE(XX')A' - AE(X)\mu' - A\mu E(X')A' + A\mu \mu' A' \\ & = AE(XX')A' - A\mu \mu' A' \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

\(\blacksquare\)

Let \(\Sigma\) be the covariance matrix. Since it’s symmetric. \(\Sigma\) can be decomposed into

\[\begin{split} \begin{align} \Sigma & = \Gamma'\Lambda\Gamma \\ & = \Gamma'\Lambda^\frac{1}{2} \Gamma \Gamma' \Lambda^\frac{1}{2} \Gamma \tag{1} \end{align} \end{split}\]

Define \(\Sigma^\frac{1}{2}\) as:

\[ \Sigma^\frac{1}{2} = \Gamma'\Lambda^\frac{1}{2} \Gamma \tag{2} \]

We can rewrite (1) as:

\[ \Sigma = \Sigma^\frac{1}{2} (\Sigma^\frac{1}{2})' = \Sigma^\frac{1}{2} \Sigma^\frac{1}{2} \]

\(\Sigma^\frac{1}{2}\) is symmetric as well as \(\Sigma\).

The inverse of \(\Sigma^\frac{1}{2}\) is:

\[ \Sigma^{-\frac{1}{2}} = \Gamma'\Lambda^{-\frac{1}{2}} \Gamma \tag{3} \]

Where \(\Gamma\) columns are orthnormal eigenvectors of \(\Lambda\), \(\Gamma\) is diagonal with eigenvalues of \(\Sigma\). that is \(\Lambda = diagonal(\lambda_1, \lambda_2,...,\lambda_n)\), \(\lambda_1 \ge \lambda_2 \ge \lambda_3 \ge ... \lambda_n \ge 0\)

Property¶

\(\Sigma\) is psd. That is \(v'\Sigma v \ge 0\)

Proof: let \(Y = v'X\), \(Var(Y) >= 0\), but \(Var(Y) = v\Sigma v'\).

\(\blacksquare\)

Moment generating function¶

Let \(Z \sim N(\mathbf{0},\mathbf{I_n})\)

Then the pdf of \(Z\) is:

\[ f(\mathbf{z}) = \left( \frac{1}{2\pi} \right)^\frac{n}{2}e^{-\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{z}'\mathbf{z}} \tag{4} \]

Let:

\[ \mathbf{X} = \Sigma^\frac{1}{2} \mathbf{Z} + \mathbf{\mu} \tag{5} \]

Then:

\[ \phi(\mathbf{t}) = E[e^{\mathbf{t'}\mathbf{X}}] = e^{\mathbf{t'}\mathbf{\mu} + \frac{1}{2} \mathbf{t'}\Sigma\mathbf{t}} \]

Rewrite (4) in terms of \(\mathbf{Z} = \Sigma^{-\frac{1}{2}}(\mathbf{X} - \mathbf{\mu})\)

The Jacobian

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} \lvert\frac{\partial{Z}}{\partial{X}}\rvert & = \lvert\Sigma^{-\frac{1}{2}}\rvert \\ & = \lvert \Gamma'\rvert \lvert \Lambda^{-\frac{1}{2}}\rvert \lvert \Gamma\rvert \\ & = 1 \times \lvert \Lambda^{-\frac{1}{2}} \rvert \times 1 \\ & = \lvert \Lambda \rvert^{-\frac{1}{2}} \\ & = \lvert \Gamma'\rvert \lvert \Lambda\rvert^{-\frac{1}{2}} \lvert \Gamma\rvert \\ & = \lvert \Sigma \rvert^{-\frac{1}{2}} \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Apply parameters transformations with the Jacobian in (4) we we get:

Density function¶

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} f(\mathbf{x}) & = \frac{1}{ \left(2\pi\right)^\frac{n}{2} \lvert\Sigma\rvert^\frac{1}{2} } e^{-\frac{1}{2}\left(\Sigma^{-\frac{1}{2}}\left(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{\mu}\right)\right)'\left(\Sigma^{-\frac{1}{2}}\left(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{\mu}\right)\right)} \\ & = \frac{1}{ \left(2\pi\right)^\frac{n}{2} \lvert\Sigma\rvert^\frac{1}{2} } e^{-\frac{1}{2}\left(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{\mu}\right)'\Sigma^{-1}\left(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{\mu}\right)} \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

How to generate Multivariate normal?¶

  • Given Covariance \(\Sigma\) and mean \(\mu\)

  • Find the eigenbases and form \(\Gamma\) and diagonal eigen value matrix \(\Gamma\). Find out \(\Sigma^\frac{1}{2}\)

  • Generate n iid std normal vector \(Z\)

  • Calculate \(X = \Sigma^\frac{1}{2}Z + \mu\)

Theorem¶

Given \(\dbinom{\vec{X_1}}{\vec{X_2}}\) normal, \(\vec{X_1}, \vec{X_2}\) independent iff \(\Sigma_{12} = 0\)

Proof: Use MGF prove \(\phi_1\times \phi_2 = \phi\)

\(\blacksquare\)

Theorem¶

\(X \sim N_n(\mathbf{\mu}, \Sigma)\), then \(\mathbf{Y} = \mathbf{A}\mathbf{X} + \mathbf{b} \sim N_m(\mathbf{A\mu+b, \mathbf{A\Sigma A'}})\) where \(A\) is \(n \times m\) mattrix.

Proof: Use MGF.

\(\blacksquare\)

Theorem¶

Suppose \(\mathbf{X} \sim N_n(\mathbf{\mu, \Sigma})\). \(\mathbf{X} = \binom{X_1}{X_2}\). Then:

\[ \mathbf{X_1}|\mathbf{X_2} \sim N_m(\mathbf{\mu_1 + \Sigma_{12}\Sigma_{22}^{-1}(X_2-\mu_2)}, \Sigma_{11}-\Sigma_{12}\Sigma_{22}^{-1}\Sigma_{21}) \]

Proof:

Write \(\mathbf{W=X_1-\Sigma_{12}\Sigma_{22}^{-1}X_2}\)

\[\begin{split} \begin{bmatrix} W \\ X_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} I_m & -\Sigma_{12}\Sigma_{22}^{-1} \\ O & I_p \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} X_1 \\ X_2 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]

Calculate Covariance of \(\begin{bmatrix} W \\ X_2 \end{bmatrix}\) indicates \(Cov(W, X_2) = 0\). Since both multinormal, \(W\) and \(X_2\) are independent. \((W+\Sigma_{12}\Sigma_{22}^{-1}X_2) | X_2\) is the same as \(X_1|X_2\).

\(\blacksquare\)
