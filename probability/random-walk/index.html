Random walk

One dimensional random walk

Youtube video link

Think of a drunk man, standing out side a bar, walk one step at a time, either left or right, with certain probability. Will he eventually return to the origin state 0? Or there’s a chance that he will never returns?

What's the average time he returns back to the bar? On average, will be be back in 10 steps, 100 steps? 1000 steps?

Time Homogeneity Property

Give any i,j∈Si, j \in Si,j∈S, P(Xn+1=j∣Xn=i)=P(X1=j∣X0=i)P(X_{n+1}=j | X_n=i) = P(X_1=j | X_0=i)P(Xn+1​=j∣Xn​=i)=P(X1​=j∣X0​=i) for any nnn. That is no matter how much time parsed, the probability of moving from state iii to jjj never changed.

Spatial Homogeneity Property

It's not only time homogeneous but also state homogeneous. i.e. P(Xn+1=j+k∣Xn=i+k)=P(X1=j∣X0=i)P(X_{n+1}=j+k | X_n=i+k) = P(X_1=j | X_0=i)P(Xn+1​=j+k∣Xn​=i+k)=P(X1​=j∣X0​=i)
